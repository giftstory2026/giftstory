# Gift Story — Channel Pricing Rules

## 1. Purpose

This file defines how Gift Story should price products across different sales channels.

The goal is to prevent pricing confusion and protect profit across:

* Salla.
* Zid.
* HungerStation.
* ToYou.
* The Chefz.
* Special Gift Story Request.
* Any future sales channel.

Each channel may have a different final price because each channel has different fees, commissions, customer behavior, and operational cost.

---

## 2. Core Pricing Rule

Gift Story does not use one fixed price for all channels.

Every price must be based on:

* Total product cost.
* Packaging cost.
* Preparation cost.
* Payment fee.
* Platform fee.
* Delivery app commission.
* Target profit.
* Channel positioning.
* Customer perception.

Minimum profit target:

```text
50% above total cost
```

Formula:

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = Total Cost × 1.5
```

This means if the total cost is 200 SAR, Gift Story should aim to receive at least 300 SAR after direct channel deductions that affect the sale.

---

## 3. Total Cost Definition

Total cost includes:

```text
Total Product Cost
+ Packaging Cost
+ Preparation Cost
+ Any Direct Extra Cost
```

Examples of direct extra costs:

* Custom ribbon.
* Extra card.
* Special protection.
* Special sourcing.
* Additional packaging material.
* Special handling cost.

---

## 4. Landed Product Cost Rule

Product cost should be recorded as landed cost.

Landed cost means the real product cost after including sourcing, shipping, customs, or travel cost if already included.

Do not count the same cost twice.

Correct:

```text
Product landed cost = real total cost of the item
```

Wrong:

```text
Product cost + shipping/customs again when already included
```

---

## 5. Packaging Cost Rule

Current working packaging assumption:

```text
18 SAR per full gift presentation
```

This includes the current basic assumption for:

* Inner box.
* Outer gift bag.
* Tissue paper.
* Sticker.
* Gift card.
* Basic finishing material.

This number must be updated if supplier pricing changes.

---

## 6. Pricing Visibility Rule

The official Gift Story website should not show detailed product prices by default.

Prices should appear inside each active sales channel only.

Reason:

Each channel may have different:

* Commission.
* Payment fee.
* Delivery fee.
* Operating cost.
* Customer expectation.
* Platform rules.
* Campaign structure.

The official website should focus on the brand, story, packaging, and direction to shop.

---

# 8. Salla / Zid Pricing

## Role

Salla or Zid is the preferred direct commerce platform.

It should handle:

* Active products.
* Prices.
* Inventory.
* Checkout.
* Payment.
* Orders.
* Invoices.
* Delivery settings.

## Direct Platform Fee Assumption

Working assumption:

```text
Payment fee = 7% + 1 SAR
```

This must be replaced by the actual payment/platform terms once confirmed.

## Direct Price Formula

If the only deduction is payment fee:

```text
Minimum Direct Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + Fixed Payment Fee) ÷ (1 - Payment Fee Percentage)
```

Using current assumption:

```text
Minimum Direct Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + 1) ÷ 0.93
```

## Example

If total cost is 180 SAR:

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = 180 × 1.5 = 270 SAR
Minimum Direct Selling Price = (270 + 1) ÷ 0.93 = 291.39 SAR
```

Recommended tier:

```text
299 SAR or 349 SAR depending on visual value and product strength
```

---

# 9. If Salla / Zid Has Extra Percentage Fees

If the commerce platform or payment gateway has additional percentage deductions, include them in the formula.

Example:

```text
Payment fee = 7%
Platform fee = 2%
Fixed fee = 1 SAR
```

Formula:

```text
Minimum Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + Fixed Fee) ÷ (1 - Total Percentage Fees)
```

Example:

```text
Minimum Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + 1) ÷ 0.91
```

Important:

Do not ignore platform fees if they are deducted from each order.

---

# 10. Delivery App Pricing

## Channels

Delivery app pricing may apply to:

* HungerStation.
* ToYou.
* The Chefz.
* Any future delivery marketplace.

## Working Commission Assumption

```text
Delivery app commission = 22.5%
```

This is a working assumption only.

It must be replaced by the actual merchant contract for each platform.

## App Price Formula

If the app deducts commission from the selling price:

```text
Minimum App Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5) ÷ (1 - App Commission)
```

Using current assumption:

```text
Minimum App Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5) ÷ 0.775
```

## Example

If total cost is 180 SAR:

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = 180 × 1.5 = 270 SAR
Minimum App Selling Price = 270 ÷ 0.775 = 348.39 SAR
```

Recommended tier:

```text
349 SAR or 399 SAR depending on channel and product value
```

---

# 11. Important Commission Warning

If a platform deducts 22.5% from the selling price, do not simply add 22.5% to the direct price.

Wrong:

```text
Direct Price × 1.225
```

Correct:

```text
Required Net Revenue ÷ 0.775
```

Reason:

Commission is deducted from the final selling price, so pricing must use division to protect profit.

---

# 12. Do Not Double Count Fees

Before setting channel price, confirm how the platform charges fees.

Ask:

```text
Does the platform deduct commission?
Does the platform also deduct payment fees?
Does the platform include payment in commission?
Is delivery charged to the customer or seller?
Are taxes included or separate?
Are there fixed order fees?
Are there monthly fees only?
```

Do not count the same fee twice.

Example:

If a delivery app commission already includes payment handling, do not add another payment fee unless the contract clearly states it.

---

# 13. Special Request Pricing

Special requests are not priced like ready products.

Special request price depends on:

* Customer request.
* Occasion.
* Products needed.
* Sourcing difficulty.
* Timeline.
* Packaging needs.
* Preparation effort.
* Delivery city.
* Budget range.
* Profit target.

Every special request must be quoted individually.

---

## Special Request Formula

Minimum quote should consider:

```text
Estimated Product Cost
+ Packaging Cost
+ Preparation Cost
+ Sourcing Cost
+ Delivery / Shipping Cost if included
+ Payment / Platform Fees
+ Target Profit
```

Minimum profit target remains:

```text
50% above total cost
```

Formula:

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = Total Estimated Cost × 1.5
```

Then add any payment or platform deduction if the payment link or platform deducts a fee.

---

# 17. 299 SAR Rule

299 SAR is allowed only when total cost supports the target profit.

It is not the fixed price for all Gift Story boxes.

Before using 299 SAR, confirm:

```text
Total cost supports 50% profit above cost
Packaging cost is included
Payment fee is included
Product value looks appropriate
Visual presentation supports the price
Channel fee does not destroy margin
```

If the box is too costly for 299 SAR, move to:

```text
349
399
449
```

depending on the cost and perceived value.

# 18. Cost Threshold Example for 299 SAR

Using direct payment assumption:

```text
Payment fee = 7% + 1 SAR
Price = 299 SAR
```

Estimated net after payment:

```text
299 × 0.93 - 1 = 277.07 SAR
```

To protect the 50% profit-above-cost rule:

```text
Total Cost × 1.5 ≤ 277.07
```

Maximum total cost approximately:

```text
184 SAR
```

Meaning:

A direct-platform product priced at 299 SAR should usually have total cost around 184 SAR or below under this fee assumption.

If total cost is higher, 299 SAR may weaken the profit target.

Different channels can carry different types of products.

## Salla / Zid

Best for:

```text
Main active drops
Full product listings
Official checkout
Better brand control
More detailed descriptions
Special fields like gift card message
```

## HungerStation / ToYou

Best for:

```text
Ready-to-go gifts
Fast local gifting
Limited simple products
Birthday or quick occasion gifts
Products easy to prepare quickly
```

## The Chefz

Best for:

```text
Premium-looking gifts
Curated limited boxes
Higher-value gifting products
Carefully selected items only
```

## Special Requests

Best for:

```text
Custom gifts
Higher budgets
Occasion-specific stories
Requests that need review
Travel-inspired or personalized ideas
```

# 22. Delivery App Product Selection Rule

Do not list every Gift Story product on delivery apps.

Delivery apps should include only:

* Ready products.
* Easy-to-prepare boxes.
* Products with clear photos.
* Products with channel-adjusted pricing.
* Items that can survive fast delivery handling.
* Items that do not require complex customization.

Avoid listing:

* Complex custom requests.
* One-off fragile boxes.
* Products needing long explanation.
* Products with weak margin after commission.
* Products that may be damaged during fast delivery.

The official website should not show active product prices by default.

The website may mention:

```text
Prices and availability are shown inside the official sales platform.
```

Arabic:

```text
الأسعار والتوفر تظهر داخل منصة البيع الرسمية.
```

Reason:

This prevents confusion when prices differ between Salla, delivery apps, and special requests.

# 24. Instagram Pricing Rule

Instagram should not be the main price catalog.

Recommended approach:

* Show story and packaging.
* Use “available through the official shop”.
* Link to website or platform.
* Avoid posting too many fixed prices.
* Do not create price confusion across channels.

If price is posted in a campaign, make sure:

```text
Channel is clear
Availability is clear
Price is still valid
Fees are included
Stock is available
```

# 25. Discount Rule

Gift Story should avoid heavy discounting.

Allowed soft offers:

```text
Launch gift
Complimentary gift card
Free delivery for selected launch area
Priority access
Limited first drop benefit
Soft add-on value
```

Avoid:

```text
Huge discount
Clearance
Flash sale
Buy one get one
Cheap price language
Heavy coupon culture
```

Gift Story should add value instead of weakening price perception.

---

# 26. Free Delivery Rule

Free delivery is allowed only if it is included in the cost calculation.

If delivery is paid by Gift Story, include it as cost.

Formula:

```text
Total Cost = Product Cost + Packaging Cost + Preparation Cost + Delivery Cost
```

Then apply:

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = Total Cost × 1.5
```

Do not offer free delivery if it destroys the target profit.

---

# 27. Campaign Price Rule

Campaign prices should be temporary and controlled.

Before approving a campaign price, check:

```text
Minimum profit is protected
Campaign duration is clear
Channel is clear
Inventory is limited
Photos are ready
Customer perception remains premium
```

Avoid campaign pricing that trains customers to wait for discounts.

---

# 28. Rounding Rule

After calculating minimum price:

1. Round up, not down.
2. Choose clean premium tier.
3. Check brand perception.
4. Check competitor/customer expectation only as a secondary factor.
5. Confirm profit after fees.

Example:

```text
Minimum calculated price = 291 SAR
Possible tiers = 299 SAR or 349 SAR
```

Choose 299 SAR only if visual value and margin are acceptable.
Choose 349 SAR if the box looks premium enough and cost pressure is higher.

---

# 31. When to Increase Price

Increase price when:

```text
Product cost increases
Packaging cost increases
Platform fee increases
Payment fee increases
Delivery app commission increases
Product value is higher
Gift story is more limited
Preparation effort is higher
Customer demand is strong
Photos and packaging support higher value
```

---

# 32. When to Reduce Cost Instead of Price

If a box becomes too expensive, first review:

```text
Product mix
Packaging waste
Unnecessary extras
Supplier cost
Channel choice
Delivery method
Preparation effort
Photo quality
Perceived value
```

Do not reduce price first.

Reducing cost while protecting presentation is usually better than weakening the brand with a low price.

---

# 33. Price Red Flags

Review price if:

```text
Profit is below 50% above cost
Platform fees are ignored
Packaging cost is missing
Delivery app commission is added incorrectly
Total cost is not landed cost
Photos do not support the price
Product looks cheaper than the price
Too many products are included without enough margin
Price differs by channel without record
Official website shows conflicting price
```

---

# 34. Channel Price Update Rule

If price changes in one channel, update the channel pricing sheet.

Record:

```text
Old price
New price
Reason for change
Channel
Date
Approved by
```

Do not change product prices randomly without updating the costing record.

---

# 35. Sold Out and Repricing Rule

If a product sells out:

```text
Mark Sold Out
Do not keep it purchasable
Move to Previous Stories if visually useful
```

If a similar new box is created:

```text
Create a new cost sheet
Create a new price
Create a new listing
```

Do not reuse old price unless the new cost supports it.

---

# 36. Final Pricing Decision

Gift Story pricing must be controlled, not emotional.

Every channel price should protect:

1. Cost accuracy.
2. Profit target.
3. Brand perception.
4. Platform fees.
5. Customer trust.
6. Operational clarity.

The correct rule is:

```text
Different channels may have different prices, but every price must be justified.
```

Gift Story should never sell a story without knowing:

```text
What it costs
What the channel deducts
What profit remains
Why the final price makes sense
```
