# Gift Story — Brand Positioning

## 1. Purpose of This File

This file defines the strategic positioning of Gift Story.

Its purpose is to make sure every future decision — website, packaging, content, pricing, product selection, customer service, and sales channels — stays aligned with the brand direction.

Gift Story should always be presented as a premium curated gifting brand, not as a normal product shop.

---

## 2. Brand Essence

Gift Story is built around one core idea:

**Every gift should feel like a story.**

The brand is not only about the items inside the box.
It is about the full emotional moment:

* The first impression.
* The packaging.
* The surprise.
* The personal message.
* The way the gift is presented.
* The feeling the receiver remembers.

Gift Story exists to make gifting feel thoughtful, elegant, and memorable.

---

## 3. Positioning Statement

Gift Story is a luxury gifting brand based in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, offering curated gift stories through limited drops and refined packaging experiences.

The brand serves customers who want a gift that looks complete, premium, and emotionally meaningful without having to build it from scratch.

Gift Story is not positioned as a discount store, a baby product shop, or a general gift marketplace.

It is positioned as:

**A curated gifting experience for meaningful occasions.**

---

## 4. What Gift Story Is

Gift Story is:

* A premium gifting brand.
* A curated gift experience.
* A storytelling-based brand.
* A limited drops concept.
* A visual and emotional gifting experience.
* A brand that values presentation as much as product selection.
* A soft luxury brand with a refined, calm, and elegant identity.

---

## 5. What Gift Story Is Not

Gift Story is not:

* A normal online shop.
* A discount gift store.
* A random product bundle business.
* A baby clothing store only.
* A flower or balloon shop.
* A fast commercial marketplace.
* A low-price product reseller.
* A brand that competes mainly on cheap pricing.
* A crowded catalog with too many unrelated items.

If the customer feels they are only buying separate products, the brand positioning has failed.

---

## 6. Core Brand Promise

Gift Story promises to deliver gifts that feel:

* Thoughtful.
* Beautifully presented.
* Ready to give.
* Emotionally meaningful.
* Carefully curated.
* Limited and special.
* Worth remembering.

Customer-facing promise:

**A gift that arrives as a complete story, not just a box of products.**

Arabic expression:

**هدية تصل كقصة كاملة، لا كمجموعة منتجات فقط.**

---

## 7. Brand Personality

Gift Story should feel:

* Elegant.
* Warm.
* Calm.
* Feminine in a refined way.
* Story-driven.
* Premium.
* Thoughtful.
* Soft.
* Organized.
* Trustworthy.
* Minimal, not empty.
* Emotional, not exaggerated.

The brand should never feel loud, cheap, rushed, or overly commercial.

---

## 8. Visual Positioning

Gift Story’s visual world should communicate quiet luxury.

The visual direction should feel:

* Off-white.
* Creamy.
* Nude.
* Muted gold.
* Clean.
* Soft.
* Spacious.
* Magical in a subtle way.
* Premium but not heavy.

Visual references:

* Castle.
* Stars.
* Moon.
* Gift card.
* Soft tissue.
* Satin gold details.
* Elegant branded packaging.

Avoid:

* Dark navy as a main identity.
* Harsh black backgrounds.
* Bright neon colors.
* Heavy glitter.
* Cartoon-style baby visuals.
* Crowded product layouts.
* Excessive flowers.
* Overly childish design.
* Cheap discount banners.
* Random icons that do not belong to the brand.

---

## 9. Tone of Voice

Gift Story’s tone should be:

* Clear.
* Warm.
* Elegant.
* Story-like.
* Practical.
* Calm.
* Confident.
* Premium without arrogance.

The writing should sound refined but still easy to understand.

The tone should not be:

* Overly salesy.
* Too casual.
* Loud.
* Full of emojis.
* Pushy.
* Artificially poetic.
* Complicated.
* Cheap or discount-focused.

---

---

## 11. Customer Perception Goal

When a customer sees Gift Story, they should think:

* This brand is organized.
* This gift looks premium.
* This is suitable for an important occasion.
* I can send this without worrying about presentation.
* The packaging itself has value.
* This feels different from a regular gift shop.
* The brand cares about details.

The customer should not think:

* These are just products placed together.
* This is a normal baby shop.
* This looks like a discount bundle.
* This brand is unclear.
* The pricing is random.
* The packaging is only decoration.

---

## 12. Target Customer

Primary target customer:

* Women in Saudi Arabia, especially Jeddah.
* Customers looking for elegant gifts.
* Friends, relatives, sisters, aunts, and colleagues.
* Customers who care about presentation.
* Customers who want a gift that looks complete.
* Customers who do not want to assemble a gift themselves.
* Customers who value premium packaging and emotional presentation.

Customer mindset:

“I want to give something that looks thoughtful, elegant, and complete.”

---

## 13. Launch Category Positioning

The first active category should be:

**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**

Arabic customer-facing name:

**هدايا الصغار**

This category should communicate:

* Softness.
* Care.
* Newborn and kids gifting.
* Elegant presentation.
* Ready-to-gift experience.
* A premium alternative to normal baby gifts.

Important note:

The category should be clear to the customer.
“Little Stories” alone is beautiful but may not be clear enough.
Therefore, it should be paired with “Baby & Kids Gifts” or its Arabic equivalent.

Recommended display format:

**Little Stories**
**Baby & Kids Gifts**

Arabic:

**هدايا الصغار**
**قصص ناعمة للمولود والطفل**

---

## 14. Coming Soon Categories

Coming Soon categories should support the larger vision of Gift Story without distracting from the launch focus.

Possible categories:

* Care Stories.
* Graduation Stories.
* Birthday Stories.
* Travel Stories.
* Special Stories.

These should appear as elegant visual cards, not as active product pages unless ready.

Each card may include:

* Category name.
* Short emotional line.
* Coming Soon label.
* Soft visual or conceptual image.

These cards should not include:

* Prices.
* Fake products.
* Exact dates unless confirmed.
* Promises that are not ready operationally.

---

## 15. Differentiation From Traditional Gift Shops

Gift Story differs from traditional gift shops in five ways:

### 1. Story Before Product

A traditional shop sells items.
Gift Story sells a curated story around the occasion.

### 2. Packaging as Part of the Value

The box, bag, card, tissue, sticker, and opening experience are part of what the customer pays for.

### 3. Limited Drops

Gift Story does not need to show endless inventory.
Limited drops create focus, quality, and desire.

### 4. Visual Consistency

Every gift must feel connected to the brand identity.

### 5. Emotional Presentation

The gift should feel personal, thoughtful, and memorable.

---

## 16. Pricing Positioning

Gift Story should not compete through low prices.

Pricing should reflect:

* Product cost.
* Packaging quality.
* Curation effort.
* Brand presentation.
* Platform fees.
* Payment fees.
* Desired profit.
* Limited availability.
* Customer experience.

The brand should avoid public messaging that makes price the main reason to buy.

Preferred value message:

**You are not paying only for products. You are paying for a complete gifting experience.**

Arabic expression:

**القيمة ليست في القطع وحدها، بل في التجربة الكاملة التي تصل بها الهدية.**

---

## 17. Website Positioning

The official website should act as the brand home.

It should show:

* The brand story.
* Gift categories.
* Packaging experience.
* Current story.
* Coming soon stories.
* Previous or sample stories.
* Special Gift Story Request.
* Policies.
* Link to the sales platform.

The website should not feel like a basic checkout page.

The website should create trust before the customer moves to the sales platform.

---

## 18. Commerce Platform Positioning

Salla, Zid, or any sales platform should be treated as the operational checkout layer.

The commerce platform handles:

* Product availability.
* Pricing.
* Orders.
* Inventory.
* Payments.
* Invoices.
* Active drops.

The official website builds desire and trust.
The commerce platform completes the transaction.

---

## 19. Special Requests Positioning

Special requests should feel premium, not like a casual custom order.

Preferred name:

**Special Gift Story Request**

Arabic:

**طلب قصة خاصة**

This feature should be positioned as:

* A curated request.
* A personalized gifting direction.
* A slower, more thoughtful process.
* Subject to availability.
* Not instant.
* Not guaranteed for every request.

Important customer note:

**Special requests require additional preparation time and depend on product availability.**

Arabic:

**الطلبات الخاصة تحتاج وقتًا إضافيًا للتجهيز وتعتمد على توفر القطع المناسبة.**

---

## 20. Content Pillars

Gift Story content should be built around these pillars:

### 1. The Story

Why this gift exists and what feeling it carries.

### 2. The Packaging

How the gift arrives and how it feels when opened.

### 3. The Details

Cards, tissue, sticker, ribbon, arrangement, and finishing.

### 4. The Drop

Limited availability and curated selection.

### 5. The Occasion

Newborn, kids, graduation, birthday, care, travel, or special request.

### 6. The Journey

From idea, sourcing, styling, preparation, packaging, and delivery.

## 23. Brand Boundaries

Gift Story should not accept or create any gift concept that:

* Looks cheap.
* Feels random.
* Does not match the visual identity.
* Cannot be presented elegantly.
* Has poor packaging fit.
* Creates operational confusion.
* Reduces the premium perception.
* Depends only on discounting.
* Requires overpromising.

If a product is good but does not fit the story or presentation, it should not be forced into Gift Story.

---

## 24. Strategic Decision

Gift Story should grow as a brand of curated stories, not as a catalog of unrelated products.

The correct growth path is:

1. Start with one clear category.
2. Build trust through visuals and packaging.
3. Sell active drops through a commerce platform.
4. Keep the website as the brand home.
5. Add categories gradually.
6. Use Coming Soon cards to show the vision without overpromising.
7. Protect pricing and presentation.
8. Keep every product tied to a story.

---

## 25. Final Positioning Summary

Gift Story is a premium Saudi gifting brand that transforms selected products into complete gift stories through elegant packaging, limited drops, and refined presentation.

It starts with:

**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**

and grows into a wider world of curated gifting stories for special occasions.

The brand should always be clear, elegant, warm, and premium.

Gift Story does not sell things.

Gift Story creates the moment around the gift.
