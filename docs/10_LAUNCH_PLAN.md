# Gift Story — Launch Plan

## 1. Purpose of This File

This file defines the launch plan for Gift Story.

Its purpose is to turn the brand foundation into a practical launch path without rushing, overbuilding, or opening too many sales directions at once.

Gift Story should launch in a controlled and premium way.

The goal is not to appear big quickly.
The goal is to appear clear, trusted, elegant, and operationally ready.

---

## 2. Launch Principle

Gift Story should launch with focus.

The first launch should not try to sell every category.

The correct launch direction is:

* One clear active category.
* One professional brand website.
* One commerce platform for sales.
* One clean pricing system.
* One clear packaging system.
* One special request form.
* Coming Soon cards for future categories.
* Controlled content and photography.
* No public WhatsApp-based selling as the main system.

---

## 3. First Launch Focus

The first public launch focus is:

**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**
**Arabic:** هدايا الصغار

Reason:

* It matches the current product direction.
* It is easier for customers to understand.
* It fits Gift Story’s soft visual identity.
* It is suitable for premium gifting.
* It allows the brand to build trust before expanding.
* It is easier to photograph and present clearly.

---

## 4. Launch Goal

The goal of the first launch is to validate:

* Customer interest.
* Packaging appeal.
* Website clarity.
* Sales platform checkout.
* Pricing acceptance.
* Product presentation.
* Operational flow.
* Delivery protection.
* Special request interest.
* Content performance.

The goal is not to sell large quantities immediately.

The launch should be treated as a controlled market test with a premium appearance.

---

## 5. Launch Scope

The first launch should include:

1. Official website.
2. Active Baby/Kids category page.
3. Packaging experience page.
4. Previous/sample stories page.
5. Special Gift Story Request page.
6. Policies page.
7. Contact page.
8. Commerce platform setup.
9. Active product/drop listings.
10. Clean product photos.
11. Packaging photos or mockups.
12. Launch content for Instagram/TikTok.
13. Basic operational workflow.
14. Pricing sheet for every active box.
15. Delivery protection process.

---

## 6. What Should Be Delayed

The following should be delayed until the first launch system works:

* Full multi-category store.
* Heavy influencer campaigns.
* Large packaging production quantities.
* Too many active drops.
* Complex custom order promises.
* Public WhatsApp ordering.
* Showing prices on the official website.
* Opening all Coming Soon categories for sale.
* Large paid advertising budget.
* Advanced backend development.
* Complicated loyalty or discount systems.
* Too many delivery app products.
* Corporate gifting pages.
* Full bilingual website if it delays launch.

---

## 7. Launch Phases

The launch should happen in six phases:

1. Foundation Setup.
2. Assets and Packaging Setup.
3. Website Setup.
4. Commerce Platform Setup.
5. Content and Product Preparation.
6. Soft Launch and Measurement.

## 9. Phase 2 — Assets and Packaging Setup

### Objective

Prepare the visual and packaging assets needed for launch.

### Tasks

* Organize logo files.
* Organize castle files.
* Organize pattern files.
* Organize sticker files.
* Organize gift card files.
* Organize box files.
* Organize outer gift bag files.
* Create preview PNG/PDF for each important asset.
* Prepare packaging mockups.
* Prepare real packaging photos when available.
* Confirm print files follow one-color muted/satin gold rule.
* Confirm dimensions:

  * Inner box: 40 × 30 × 10 cm.
  * Outer gift bag: 45 × 33 × 15 cm.
* Confirm protective nylon/shipping carton approach.

### Completion Criteria

Phase 2 is complete when all brand assets are easy to preview, correctly named, and mapped to their usage.

---

## 10. Phase 3 — Website Setup

### Objective

Build the official Gift Story website as a premium brand home.

### Required Website Pages

* Home.
* Our Story.
* Little Stories.
* Packaging.
* Previous Stories.
* Special Request.
* Policies.
* Contact.
* Shop redirect page or button.

### Main CTA

**English:** Shop The Current Story
**Arabic:** تسوّقي القصة الحالية

### Website Must Show

* Gift Story brand concept.
* Little Stories category.
* Packaging experience.
* Coming Soon categories.
* Previous/sample stories.
* Special Gift Story Request.
* Policies.
* Clear direction to sales platform.

### Website Must Not Show

* Detailed product prices by default.
* Conflicting channel prices.
* Public WhatsApp as main order method.
* Too many unfinished categories.
* Low-quality product images.
* Raw inventory photos.
* Retail price tags.
* Discount-focused language.

### Completion Criteria

Phase 3 is complete when the official website looks premium, works well on mobile, and directs customers clearly to the sales platform.

---

## 11. Phase 4 — Commerce Platform Setup

### Objective

Set up the sales platform for actual orders, payments, inventory, and checkout.

### Preferred Starting Platform

**Salla**, unless Zid is selected after comparison.

### Tasks

* Choose Salla or Zid.
* Configure store name and branding.
* Add active products only.
* Add product photos.
* Add product descriptions.
* Add inventory quantities.
* Add delivery/preparation notes.
* Add policy notes.
* Configure payment methods.
* Configure delivery methods.
* Test checkout.
* Test payment.
* Test order notification.
* Test customer experience.
* Connect shop button from official website.

### Product Listing Rule

Only active and ready products should be listed.

Coming Soon categories should not be listed for sale until products are ready.

### Completion Criteria

Phase 4 is complete when a customer can place an order through the platform without manual WhatsApp handling.

---

### Objective

### Tasks

* Add packaging cost.

* Apply 50% profit-above-cost rule.
* Calculate platform/payment fees.
* Approve final price per channel.
* Prepare product photos.
* Prepare packaging photos.
* Prepare website visuals.
* Prepare social content.
* Prepare launch captions.
* Prepare customer FAQ.
* Prepare Special Request form.
* Prepare order preparation checklist.

### Required Content

* Brand intro post.
* Packaging experience post.
* Little Stories category post.
* Current story launch post.
* Special Gift Story Request post.
* Coming Soon teaser.
* Behind-the-scenes content.
* Product photo set.
* Short vertical video.
* Story templates.

### Completion Criteria

Phase 5 is complete when the first launch products and content are ready to be shown publicly.

---

## 13. Phase 6 — Soft Launch

### Objective

Launch carefully and measure real customer response.

### Recommended Soft Launch

* Start with Baby/Kids focus.
* Use limited active products.
* Keep quantities controlled.
* Test website flow.
* Test sales platform checkout.
* Test packaging preparation.
* Test delivery protection.
* Collect customer feedback.
* Track pricing acceptance.
* Track special request interest.

### Duration

Recommended first soft launch period:

**14 to 30 days**

This period is enough to test customer behavior without committing to large quantities.

---

## 14. Launch Quantity

The first quantity should stay controlled.

Recommended starting point:

**Small controlled drop**

If inventory is already available, do not force a large production quantity.

The better approach is:

* Prepare enough to test demand.
* Avoid overstock.
* Avoid large packaging commitments before seeing response.
* Use early orders to improve product selection and photos.

Suggested working range:

**20 to 30 ready gift sets or box concepts**, depending on inventory readiness.

Important:

Do not launch more than operations can prepare beautifully.

---

## 15. Launch Financial Rule

Every box must be costed before sale.

Required before approving any launch price:

* Landed product cost.
* Packaging cost.
* Platform/payment fee.
* Delivery app fee if applicable.
* Preparation cost if applicable.
* Target profit.
* Final sales channel price.

Minimum profit target:

**50% above total cost**

No product should go live without a costing sheet.

---

* Brand trust.

* Visual identity.
* Current story direction.
* Special request page.

### Commerce Platform

Used for:

* Price.
* Inventory.
* Purchase.
* Payment.
* Orders.

### Instagram / TikTok

Used for:

* Discovery.
* Content.
* Trust.
* Visual storytelling.
* Directing customers to the website or shop.

Used only as secondary support when needed.

### Delivery Apps

Used later or selectively, not as the first main system unless products are operationally simple.

## 24. Success Criteria

The launch is considered successful if:

* Customers understand what Gift Story is.
* Website looks premium and credible.
* Customers click to shop.
* Sales platform works smoothly.
* Orders can be fulfilled without chaos.
* Packaging arrives clean.
* Customers respond positively to visuals.
* Pricing does not feel confusing.
* Special request interest appears.
* The brand can identify which products and categories deserve expansion.

---

## 25. Warning Signs

Review the launch if:

* Customers ask “What exactly do you sell?”
* Customers cannot find where to order.
* Prices are being questioned too often.
* Packaging photos look weak.
* Product photos do not support premium pricing.
* Orders require too much manual explanation.
* Customers expect WhatsApp ordering only.
* Special requests become unmanageable.
* Delivery damages packaging.
* Social content looks generic.
* The website feels empty or unfinished.
* The sales platform looks disconnected from the brand.

---

## 26. First Month Decision Points

At the end of the first 14 to 30 days, decide:

1. Should Little Stories continue as the main category?
2. Which products sold or attracted interest?
3. Which price tiers worked?
4. Is Salla/Zid working smoothly?
5. Are special requests worth keeping active?
6. Are Coming Soon categories helping or distracting?
7. Is packaging cost accurate?
8. Are photos strong enough?
9. Should delivery apps be opened?
10. Should the next drop be Baby/Kids, Birthday, Care, Graduation, or Travel?

---

## 27. What to Improve After Soft Launch

After the first launch, improve:

* Product photography.
* Packaging photography.
* Product costing sheets.
* Website visuals.
* Commerce platform product pages.
* Special request form.
* FAQ.
* Delivery protection.
* Content style.
* Category cards.
* Customer support templates.

Do not scale ads aggressively before these basics are strong.

---

## 28. Paid Ads Rule

Paid ads should start small.

Do not spend heavily before:

* Website is ready.
* Product photos are strong.
* Commerce checkout works.
* Pricing is approved.
* Packaging is ready.
* Fulfillment is tested.

Initial paid testing should focus on:

* Little Stories.
* Packaging experience.
* Gift-ready concept.
* Premium baby/kids gifting.
* Special request interest if relevant.

Avoid discount-led ads.

---

## 29. Launch Decision

Gift Story should launch softly, not loudly.

Recommended launch style:

* Premium.
* Calm.
* Visual.
* Controlled.
* Focused.
* One active direction.
* Strong packaging story.
* Official checkout.
* Clear special request option.
* Coming Soon vision.

The launch should say:

Gift Story is ready, but intentionally limited.

---

## 30. Final Launch Summary

Gift Story’s first launch should focus on:

**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**

The official website should act as the brand home.
The commerce platform should handle sales and payments.
Special requests should use a dedicated form.
Prices should appear inside sales channels only.
Packaging should be protected and presented beautifully.
Future categories should appear as Coming Soon visual cards.

The goal is to launch a clear, premium, and controlled brand experience before expanding.
