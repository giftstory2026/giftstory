# Gift Story — Categories and Drops

## 1. Purpose of This File

This file defines the category and drop structure for Gift Story.

Its purpose is to prevent the brand from becoming a random product catalog and to keep every product, box, and collection connected to a clear story.

Gift Story should grow through curated categories and limited drops, not through uncontrolled product listings.

---

## 2. Category Strategy

Gift Story should be structured around gift stories, not ordinary product categories.

The customer should feel that each category represents an occasion, emotion, or gifting moment.

The category system should be:

* Clear to the customer.
* Elegant in wording.
* Easy to expand.
* Suitable for the official website.
* Suitable for Salla / Zid or any commerce platform.
* Flexible enough for seasonal and limited drops.
* Not crowded during launch.

---

## 3. Launch Category

The first active category is:

**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**
**Arabic Name:** هدايا الصغار

This category covers gifts for:

* Newborns.
* Babies.
* Young children.
* Baby girls.
* Baby boys.
* Soft baby/kids gift sets.
* Elegant kids occasion gifts.

This category is the correct first launch focus because it matches the currently available product direction and is easier to explain visually and commercially.

---

## 4. Customer-Facing Category Display

Recommended display format on the official website:

**هدايا الصغار**
**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**

Short Arabic line:

**قصص ناعمة للمولود والطفل، منسقة بعناية لتصل كهدية كاملة.**

Short English support line:

**Curated baby and kids gifts, wrapped as soft little stories.**

Important note:

“Little Stories” is elegant but may not be clear enough alone.
It should always be paired with “Baby & Kids Gifts” or the Arabic name “هدايا الصغار” for clarity.

---

## 5. First Active Drop

The first active drop should be under:

**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**

Possible internal drop name:

**Little Stories Drop 01**

Possible Arabic customer-facing name:

**القصة الأولى للصغار**

This drop can include baby and kids gift boxes depending on available inventory.

It should not be limited only to newborns unless the actual products are only newborn-focused.

---

## 6. Active vs Coming Soon

Gift Story should separate categories into three status levels:

### 1. Active

Ready for display, pricing, sale, and fulfillment through the commerce platform.

### 2. Coming Soon

Visible on the official website as a soft visual card, but not open for purchase yet.

### 3. Request-Based

Available only through a form or manual review, not as a normal instant product.

## 8. Coming Soon Cards

Coming Soon categories should appear on the official website as elegant visual cards.

Each card may include:

* Category name.
* Arabic name.
* Short emotional description.
* Soft visual concept.
* Coming Soon label.

Coming Soon cards should not include:

* Prices.
* Exact product promises.
* Specific quantities.
* Exact launch dates unless confirmed.
* Items that are not sourced or ready.
* Strong sales language.

The goal is to show the future world of Gift Story without confusing the current launch focus.

## 10. Special Gift Story Request

Special requests should have a dedicated page, not just a small contact form.

**English Page Name:** Special Gift Story Request
**Arabic Name:** طلب قصة خاصة

This page is used for:

* Custom gift boxes.
* Travel-related gift requests.
* Pre-travel sourcing requests.
* Special occasions.
* Personalized gift concepts.
* Requests that need time and manual review.

Important customer-facing note:

**Arabic:**
الطلبات الخاصة تحتاج وقتًا إضافيًا للتجهيز، وتعتمد على توفر القطع المناسبة وإمكانية تنفيذ الفكرة.

**English:**
Special requests require additional preparation time and depend on product availability.

---

## 11. Drop Definition

A Gift Story drop is not just a group of products.

A drop must include:

* A clear story or occasion.
* A defined category.
* A limited quantity.
* A visual direction.
* A product cost calculation.
* A pricing decision per sales channel.
* A packaging fit check.
* A content plan.
* A fulfillment plan.

If a group of products has no story, it should not be launched as a Gift Story drop.

---

## 12. Drop Requirements Before Launch

Before any drop goes live, the following must be ready:

1. Drop name.
2. Category.
3. Target customer.
4. Occasion.
5. Product list.
6. Actual product cost.
7. Packaging cost.
8. Platform/payment fee estimate.
9. Target profit.
10. Final platform price.
11. Quantity available.
12. Product photos.
13. Packaging photos or mockups.
14. Customer-facing description.
15. Internal fulfillment checklist.
16. Delivery method.
17. Policy note.
18. Launch content.

---

## 13. Pricing Rule for Drops

Prices should not be shown on the official brand website by default.

Prices should be shown only inside active sales channels, such as:

* Salla.
* Zid.
* HungerStation.
* ToYou.
* The Chefz.
* Other approved commerce or delivery platforms.

Each channel may have a different price depending on:

* Platform commission.
* Payment fees.
* Delivery fees.
* Operational complexity.
* Promotion rules.
* Channel positioning.

---

## 14. Official Website Role for Drops

The official website should show:

* Active category.
* Current story.
* Visual presentation.
* Packaging experience.
* Coming Soon categories.
* Previous/sample stories.
* Special request page.
* Button to the sales platform.

The official website should not become a crowded product catalog.

Its purpose is to build trust and desire before the customer purchases through the commerce platform.

---

## 15. Commerce Platform Role for Drops

The commerce platform should show:

* Active products.
* Actual prices.
* Product availability.
* Inventory quantity.
* Product photos.
* Purchase button.
* Payment options.
* Delivery options.
* Order confirmation.
* Customer invoices.

The commerce platform is the operational sales layer.

The official website is the brand and storytelling layer.

---

## 16. Category Naming Rules

Category names should be:

* Elegant.
* Easy to understand.
* Connected to gifting.
* Flexible for future drops.
* Not too childish.
* Not too generic.
* Not too commercial.

Avoid category names that feel:

* Cheap.
* Random.
* Too playful for a luxury brand.
* Too unclear.
* Too long.
* Too similar to a discount bundle.

---

## 17. Drop Naming Rules

Drop names should feel like a story or occasion.

Good formats:

* Little Stories Drop 01.
* The First Little Story.
* A New Arrival Story.
* The Cupcake Birthday Drop.
* Graduation Story 01.
* The Travel Story: Paris.
* The Care Story: Soft Morning.

Arabic examples:

* القصة الأولى للصغار.
* قصة المولود الناعمة.
* قصة تخرج ناعمة.
* قصة عيد الميلاد.
* قصة من السفر.
* قصة عناية هادئة.

Avoid:

* باقة رقم 1.
* عرض أطفال.
* بوكس عشوائي.
* أرخص بوكس.
* مجموعة مخفضة.
* منتجات متنوعة.
* تشكيلة أطفال فقط.

---

## 18. Inventory Rule

Each drop should be connected to real available inventory.

Do not announce a drop unless:

* Products are available or secured.
* Quantities are known.
* Costs are calculated.
* Photos can be prepared.
* Packaging can fit the products.
* Fulfillment is possible.

Coming Soon categories can be shown as brand vision, but active drops must be operationally real.

---

## 19. Product Fit Rule

A product can be included in Gift Story only if it fits:

* The category.
* The occasion.
* The brand identity.
* The box size.
* The packaging style.
* The target customer.
* The pricing model.
* The premium perception.

If a product is useful but does not look elegant inside the box, it should not be forced into a drop.

---

## 20. Visual Rule for Categories

Each category should have a visual direction.

### Little Stories

Soft, clean, baby/kids, off-white, creamy, gentle, warm.

### Care Stories

Soft care, calm textures, spa-like but not generic.

### Graduation Stories

Elegant celebration, achievement, minimal gold, refined.

### Birthday Stories

Soft celebration, cake/cupcake inspiration, not childish or loud.

### Travel Stories

Curated, cultural, destination-inspired, premium souvenir feeling.

### Special Stories

Personal, elegant, custom, quiet luxury.

---

## 21. Launch Priority

The launch priority should be:

1. Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts.
2. Special Gift Story Request page.
3. Coming Soon cards for future categories.
4. Commerce platform setup.
5. Product photos and packaging visuals.
6. Drop content and launch campaign.

Do not open too many active categories at the beginning.

The brand should look rich in vision, but focused in execution.

---

## 22. Categories to Delay

The following should remain Coming Soon until operationally ready:

* Care Stories.
* Graduation Stories.
* Birthday Stories.
* Travel Stories.

Do not launch these categories unless:

* Products are available.
* Pricing is calculated.
* Photos are ready.
* Fulfillment process is clear.
* Packaging works.
* Sales channel is prepared.

---

---

## 24. Final Category Decision

The first public direction for Gift Story is:

**هدايا الصغار**
**Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts**

Other categories should appear as elegant Coming Soon cards to show the brand’s future direction without distracting from the first launch.

Gift Story should grow slowly, clearly, and beautifully.

The brand should not rush to become a marketplace.

It should become a world of curated stories.
