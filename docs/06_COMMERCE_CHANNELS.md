# Gift Story — Commerce Channels

## 1. Purpose of This File

This file defines the sales and commerce channel strategy for Gift Story.

Its purpose is to separate the role of the official brand website from the role of sales platforms, delivery apps, forms, customer support, and payment channels.

Gift Story should look organized and premium in front of customers.
The customer journey should feel clear, official, and trustworthy.

---

## 2. Core Commerce Principle

Gift Story should not operate like a random WhatsApp-based seller.

The preferred customer journey is:

1. Customer discovers Gift Story through the official website or social media.
2. Customer explores the brand, categories, packaging, and previous stories.
3. Customer clicks the main shopping button.
4. Customer is directed to the active commerce platform.
5. Customer completes the order through an official checkout.
6. Gift Story prepares and delivers the gift according to the approved operations process.

The brand should feel official before the customer pays.

---

| Channel              | Role                                                                           |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Official Website     | Brand home, storytelling, categories, visuals, previous stories, request forms |
| Salla / Zid          | Main sales platform, products, prices, inventory, payment, orders              |
| HungerStation        | Fast delivery / local discovery channel when suitable                          |
| ToYou                | Delivery app channel when suitable                                             |
| The Chefz            | Premium app channel if suitable for gifting products                           |

| WhatsApp             | Secondary support only, not the main public sales channel                      |

---

## 4. Official Website Role

The official website should be the main brand home.

Recommended domain:

```text
gift-story.com
```

The website should focus on:

* Brand story.
* Gift Story concept.
* Packaging experience.
* Little Stories — Baby & Kids Gifts.
* Coming Soon categories.
* Previous or sample gift stories.
* Special Gift Story Request page.
* Policies.
* Contact.
* Shop The Current Story button.

The official website should not look like a basic checkout page.

It should create desire, trust, and premium perception before sending the customer to the active sales platform.

---

## 5. Official Website Pricing Rule

The official website should not show detailed active product prices by default.

Reason:

Each sales channel may have a different price due to:

* Payment fees.
* Platform commission.
* Delivery app commission.
* Channel positioning.
* Operational cost.
* Promotion rules.
* Inventory strategy.

Prices should appear inside the active sales channel only.

Examples:

* Salla price.
* Zid price.
* HungerStation price.
* ToYou price.
* The Chefz price.
* Custom quote price.

This prevents customer confusion when prices differ between platforms.

## 7. Salla / Zid Role

Salla or Zid should handle the operational sales layer.

The selected commerce platform should manage:

* Product listings.
* Active drop pricing.
* Inventory.
* Checkout.
* Payment gateways.
* Customer details.
* Orders.
* Invoices.
* Delivery settings.
* Discount rules if needed.
* Product availability.
* Taxes if applicable.
* Customer notifications.

The platform should be treated as the official purchasing environment.

---

## 8. Preferred Starting Platform

The preferred starting direction is:

**Salla**

Reason:

* It is suitable for a fast Saudi launch.
* It is familiar to many local customers.
* It supports structured product listings and checkout.
* It can manage inventory and orders more professionally than manual WhatsApp selling.
* It can act as the sales engine while the official website remains the brand home.

However, the final decision between Salla and Zid should be made after checking:

* Monthly cost.
* Payment fees.
* Checkout experience.
* Design flexibility.
* Domain/subdomain support.
* Product options and variants.
* Delivery integrations.
* Inventory management.
* App integrations.
* Customer notification options.
* Ease of daily operation.

If Zid becomes operationally easier or more suitable later, the commerce file can be updated.

## 9. Domain Strategy

The official website should remain:

```text
gift-story.com
```

The sales platform can be connected in one of the following ways:

### Option A — Recommended Starting Option

```text
shop.gift-story.com
```

This is usually cleaner and easier if the commerce platform supports subdomains.

### Option B — Preferred Visual Option If Technically Supported

```text
gift-story.com/orders
```

This looks elegant and may feel more unified, but it may require technical routing that is not always supported directly by commerce platforms.

Important rule:

Do not force `/orders` if it creates technical instability, SEO issues, checkout problems, or platform limitations.

Practical recommendation:

Start with a supported subdomain if needed.
Keep the official website as the SEO and brand home.

## 13. Payment Strategy

Customer payments should happen through official and professional checkout methods.

Preferred payment methods:

* Mada.
* Visa / Mastercard.
* Apple Pay if available.
* Tabby / Tamara if approved and suitable.
* Platform-supported online payment methods.

Direct bank transfer should not be the default public-facing method, even if it is cheaper.

Reason:

A professional checkout experience increases trust and protects the premium image of Gift Story.

---

## 14. Bank Transfer Rule

Bank transfer may exist only as an internal or exceptional method.

It should not be promoted as the main customer payment method on the official website.

Possible use cases:

* Internal business payments.
* Platform settlements.
* Special cases approved manually.
* Operational needs behind the scenes.

Customer-facing priority should remain official checkout.

---

## 15. WhatsApp Role

WhatsApp should not be the main public ordering channel.

It may be used only for:

* Selected customer support cases.
* Manual clarification.
* After an email inquiry.
* Complex special request follow-up.
* Delivery coordination when needed.
* Customers who specifically need direct conversation.

Recommended customer flow:

1. Customer submits a form or inquiry.
2. Gift Story reviews the request.
3. Gift Story replies by email.
4. If WhatsApp is needed, Gift Story shares the WhatsApp contact selectively.

This keeps the brand more formal and organized.

## 18. Special Request Form Status

The Special Gift Story Request form can be handled in one of three ways:

### 1. Always Active

Good for collecting opportunities and custom inquiries.

### 2. Campaign Active

Useful before travel, seasonal sourcing, or limited custom windows.

### 3. Paused

Used when capacity is full or custom requests cannot be handled.

If paused, the page should show a premium message instead of disappearing.

Example:

**Arabic:**

طلبات القصص الخاصة غير متاحة حاليًا. نعمل على تجهيز قصص جديدة، ويمكنك متابعة الإصدارات القادمة قريبًا.

## 19. Special Request Customer Note

**Arabic:**

## 21. Delivery App Channels

Delivery apps may be used for selected categories.

Potential platforms:

* HungerStation.
* ToYou.
* The Chefz.
* Other suitable local platforms.

These channels should be used carefully because they may:

* Charge commissions.
* Change customer expectations.
* Encourage faster purchase behavior.
* Require different pricing.
* Affect packaging protection.
* Increase operational complexity.

Delivery app products should be curated and limited, not the full Gift Story world.

---

## 22. Delivery App Pricing Rule

Delivery app pricing must be calculated separately.

Working assumption until contracts are confirmed:

```text
Delivery app commission assumption = 22.5%
```

This number must be replaced by the actual merchant contract terms once available.

Important:

If the platform deducts a commission from the selling price, the price must be calculated by dividing by the remaining percentage, not simply adding the commission on top.

Example:

```text
If commission is 22.5%, remaining revenue is 77.5%.
```

```text
Minimum App Price = Required Net Revenue ÷ 0.775
```

Each channel can have a different price.

Example:

| Channel          | Pricing Logic                               |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Salla / Zid      | Direct platform price                       |
| HungerStation    | App commission-adjusted price               |
| ToYou            | App commission-adjusted price               |
| The Chefz        | Premium app-adjusted price                  |
| Special Request  | Custom quote                                |
| Official Website | No detailed public product price by default |

The customer should not see inconsistent prices on the official brand website.

## 24. Channel Product Selection

Not every Gift Story product should be available on every channel.

Recommended logic:

### Salla / Zid

Best for:

* Main active drops.
* Full product listings.
* Inventory management.
* Official checkout.

### HungerStation / ToYou

Best for:

* Fast local gifting.
* Birthday or quick occasion gifts.
* Selected ready-to-go items.
* Higher price due to platform commission.

### The Chefz

Best for:

* Premium gifting if the platform audience fits.
* Selected luxury-looking boxes.
* Limited curated items.

### Official Website

Best for:

* Brand trust.
* Storytelling.
* Visual archive.
* Special request entry.
* Redirecting to purchase.

## 25. Customer Journey — Normal Purchase

Recommended journey:

1. Customer sees Gift Story on Instagram, search, or direct link.
2. Customer enters official website.
3. Customer explores the brand and packaging.
4. Customer clicks Shop The Current Story.
5. Customer opens the commerce platform.
6. Customer selects the active product.
7. Customer pays through official checkout.
8. Gift Story receives the order.
9. Gift Story prepares the box.
10. Customer receives the gift.

---

## 26. Customer Journey — Special Request

Recommended journey:

1. Customer enters Special Gift Story Request page.
2. Customer submits the form.
3. Gift Story reviews the request.
4. Gift Story replies by email.
5. If needed, WhatsApp is shared selectively.
6. Gift concept and price are confirmed.
7. Payment is completed through an official method or payment link.
8. Gift Story prepares the custom order.
9. Order is delivered or shipped.

---

## 27. Customer Journey — Delivery App

Recommended journey:

1. Customer discovers Gift Story on delivery app.
2. Customer sees selected ready-to-order items.
3. Customer orders through the app.
4. Gift Story prepares the item quickly.
5. Order is protected for delivery.
6. Delivery partner handles pickup and drop-off.

Delivery app items should be operationally simple and suitable for fast preparation.

---

## 28. What Should Not Happen

Gift Story should avoid:

* Taking public orders only through WhatsApp.
* Showing one price on the official website and another price on platforms without explanation.
* Using bank transfer as the main visible payment method.
* Listing too many unrelated products in apps.
* Opening custom requests without capacity.
* Promising delivery times that cannot be met.
* Allowing courier labels on branded packaging.
* Creating discounts that weaken the brand.
* Treating Salla/Zid as a replacement for brand storytelling.
* Treating the official website as a basic landing page only.

## 31. Final Commerce Decision

Gift Story should operate with a two-layer system:

### Layer 1 — Official Website

Brand, story, trust, visuals, categories, packaging, previous stories, special request page.

### Layer 2 — Commerce Platform

Products, prices, inventory, checkout, payment, orders, invoices.

This structure protects the premium image of Gift Story and keeps operations organized.

Gift Story should not depend on WhatsApp as the main storefront.

The brand should look official, calm, premium, and operationally clear.
