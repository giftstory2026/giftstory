# Gift Story — Pricing Model

## 1. Purpose of This File

This file defines the pricing model for Gift Story.

Its purpose is to prevent random pricing and make sure every gift box is priced based on:

* Actual cost.
* Packaging cost.
* Sales channel fees.
* Payment fees.
* Required profit.
* Brand positioning.
* Category value.
* Customer experience.

Gift Story should not use one fixed price for all boxes.

---

## 2. Core Pricing Principle

Gift Story does not price products only.

Gift Story prices a complete gift experience.

Each price should reflect:

* The products inside the box.
* The packaging.
* The gift presentation.
* The sourcing effort.
* The styling and curation.
* The platform fees.
* The payment fees.
* The delivery channel.
* The desired profit.
* The premium perception of the brand.

---

## 3. 299 SAR Price Rule

The 299 SAR price is not cancelled.

However, it should not be treated as the fixed price for all Gift Story boxes.

299 SAR is only one possible pricing tier.

Gift Story may use several pricing tiers depending on the actual cost and category.

Possible price tiers:

* 249 SAR
* 299 SAR
* 349 SAR
* 399 SAR
* 449 SAR
* 499 SAR+
* Custom price for special requests

The final price must be calculated based on the actual box cost and the sales channel.

---

## 4. Profit Target

The minimum target profit is:

**50% above total cost**

This means:

If the total cost is 200 SAR, the minimum target profit is 100 SAR.

Formula:

```text
Target Profit = Total Cost × 50%
```

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = Total Cost + Target Profit
```

```text
Minimum Net Revenue Needed = Total Cost × 1.5
```

Important:

This is the minimum financial target.
Final selling price may be higher depending on brand positioning, platform fees, and perceived value.

---

## 5. Total Cost Definition

Total cost should include all direct costs needed to create and sell the gift.

Total cost may include:

1. Landed product cost.
2. Packaging cost.
3. Printing cost.
4. Gift card cost.
5. Sticker cost.
6. Tissue paper cost.
7. Outer gift bag cost.
8. Protective nylon or shipping protection.
9. Internal support material if needed.
10. Special preparation cost.
11. Any direct cost related to the box.

---

## 6. Landed Product Cost

Product cost should be calculated as:

**Landed Product Cost**

This means the real cost of the product after considering any included shipping, customs, travel, or sourcing cost.

If international shipping and customs are already included in the product cost, they should not be counted again as separate costs.

Example:

```text
Wrong:
Product cost + international shipping + customs again
```

```text
Correct:
Landed product cost
```

---

## 7. Packaging Cost

The current estimated packaging base cost is approximately:

**18 SAR per box**

This estimate is based on current assumptions such as:

* Box.
* Print.
* Outer gift bag.
* Tissue paper.
* Gift card.
* Sticker.
* Basic finishing materials.

This cost is not fixed forever.

Packaging cost must be recalculated per batch depending on:

* Supplier.
* Quantity.
* Printing cost.
* Reprint needs.
* Material quality.
* Packaging waste.
* Sample production.
* Protection materials.

---

## 8. Payment Fee Rule

For direct platform sales, payment fees should be included in the pricing calculation.

Working assumption:

```text
Payment fee = 7% + 1 SAR
```

This assumption should be updated later based on the confirmed payment gateway or commerce platform.

---

## 9. Direct Sales Price Formula

For sales through Salla, Zid, or another direct commerce platform:

```text
Minimum Direct Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + Fixed Payment Fee) ÷ (1 - Payment Fee Percentage)
```

Using the current working assumption:

```text
Minimum Direct Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + 1) ÷ 0.93
```

This gives the minimum price required to protect the 50% profit-above-cost target after payment fees.

---

## 11. Brand Pricing Floor

Gift Story should avoid prices that make the brand feel cheap.

Even if the formula allows a low price, the final price should still match:

* Packaging quality.
* Category positioning.
* Customer perception.
* Visual presentation.
* Occasion value.
* Sales channel.

Recommended rule:

```text
Final Price = Higher of:
1. Financial minimum price
2. Brand-appropriate price tier
3. Channel-adjusted price
```

---

## 12. Channel-Specific Pricing

Each sales channel may have a different price.

Prices may differ across:

* Salla.
* Zid.
* HungerStation.
* ToYou.
* The Chefz.
* Any future platform.
* Custom request forms.

Reason:

Each channel has different:

* Commission.
* Payment cost.
* Delivery structure.
* Customer behavior.
* Operational effort.
* Promotion rules.
* Platform positioning.

Therefore, the official brand website should not show detailed product prices by default.

---

## 13. Delivery App Commission Rule

Delivery apps may charge commissions or fees.

Working assumption until contract confirmation:

```text
Delivery app commission assumption = 22.5%
```

Important:

This number must be verified through the actual merchant contract.

Do not treat public estimates or informal numbers as final.

---

## 14. Delivery App Pricing Formula

If a delivery app deducts a percentage commission from the sale price, use this formula:

```text
Minimum App Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5) ÷ (1 - App Commission Percentage)
```

If there is also a fixed payment or platform fee:

```text
Minimum App Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5 + Fixed Fee) ÷ (1 - Total Percentage Fees)
```

Using the 22.5% working assumption:

```text
Minimum App Selling Price = (Total Cost × 1.5) ÷ 0.775
```

Important warning:

Adding 22.5% on top of the direct price is not always enough if the platform deducts 22.5% from the sale price.

If the platform deducts commission, pricing must use division, not simple addition.

## 16. Price Rounding Rule

Gift Story should use clean premium pricing.

Recommended endings:

* 249
* 299
* 349
* 399
* 449
* 499
* 549
* 599
* 649
* 699

Avoid messy prices unless required by a platform.

Avoid:

* 287
* 312
* 376
* 418
* 463

Clean pricing feels more premium and easier to understand.

---

## 17. Salla / Zid Pricing Role

Salla or Zid should be used for:

* Active product pricing.
* Inventory.
* Checkout.
* Payment.
* Orders.
* Invoices.
* Customer purchase flow.

The official website should direct customers to the active sales platform using:

**Shop The Current Story**

Arabic:

**تسوّقي القصة الحالية**

Prices should appear inside the sales platform, not on the main brand website by default.

---

## 18. Official Website Pricing Rule

The official Gift Story website should not show detailed product prices by default.

The website should focus on:

* Brand story.
* Gift categories.
* Packaging experience.
* Previous stories.
* Current story teaser.
* Coming Soon cards.
* Special Gift Story Request.
* Link to sales platform.

This allows different platforms to have different prices without confusing customers.

---

## 19. Special Request Pricing

Special requests should not use fixed public pricing.

Special request pricing depends on:

* Occasion.
* Product type.
* Sourcing difficulty.
* Preparation time.
* Packaging needs.
* Customization level.
* Customer budget.
* Delivery timeline.

Recommended approach:

1. Customer submits the form.
2. Gift Story reviews the request.
3. A concept and price range are prepared.
4. Customer confirms.
5. Payment is completed through an official method or platform link.
6. Order is prepared.

Special requests should clearly state:

**Special requests require additional preparation time and depend on product availability.**

---

## 20. Inventory Costing Rule

Each product added to inventory should have:

* Product name.
* Category.
* Brand.
* Source.
* Landed cost.
* Quantity.
* Condition.
* Size / age group if applicable.
* Suitable category.
* Suggested box usage.
* Notes.
* Image filename.
* Internal value.
* Retail value if relevant.
* Recommended selling tier.

No product should be added to a paid gift box without cost tracking.

---

## 21. Box Costing Rule

Each box should have its own cost sheet.

Required fields:

* Box name.
* Category.
* Drop name.
* Product list.
* Landed cost of each product.
* Total product cost.
* Packaging cost.
* Special preparation cost.
* Platform fee assumption.
* Payment fee assumption.
* Total cost.
* Minimum direct price.
* Suggested direct price.
* Minimum app price.
* Suggested app price.
* Target profit.
* Final approved price.
* Approval date.

---

## 22. Pricing Workflow

Before approving any box price:

1. Add all product costs.
2. Add packaging cost.
3. Add direct preparation costs.
4. Calculate total cost.
5. Apply 50% profit-above-cost rule.
6. Add payment/platform fee calculation.
7. Determine minimum direct platform price.
8. Determine minimum delivery app price if needed.
9. Select a clean premium price tier.
10. Check if the price matches customer perception.
11. Approve final price per channel.

## 24. Discount Policy

Gift Story should avoid strong discount language.

Allowed:

* Launch gift.
* Free delivery for a limited area or campaign.
* Complimentary gift card.
* Limited first drop benefit.
* Priority access.
* Soft value-added offer.

Avoid:

* Huge discount.
* Buy one get one.
* Clearance.
* Flash sale.
* Cheap price.
* Heavy coupon culture.
* Repeated discount codes.

Gift Story should add value instead of reducing perceived value.

---

## 25. Pricing Red Flags

A price should be reviewed if:

* Profit is below 50% above cost.
* Platform fees were not included.
* Packaging cost was ignored.
* Delivery app commission was treated incorrectly.
* Product cost does not include real landed cost.
* The final price feels too low for the brand.
* The box looks too expensive compared to visual value.
* The customer cannot understand the value.
* Product photos are weak.
* Packaging does not support the price.

---

## 26. When to Increase Price

Prices should increase when:

* Product cost increases.
* Packaging cost increases.
* Platform commission increases.
* Payment fees increase.
* The box includes higher-value products.
* The story is more exclusive.
* The box requires special preparation.
* Demand is strong.
* The drop quantity is very limited.
* Photos and presentation strongly support the premium value.

---

## 27. When to Reduce Cost Instead of Price

If a box becomes too expensive, do not immediately reduce the selling price.

First review:

* Product selection.
* Packaging waste.
* Unnecessary extras.
* Supplier alternatives.
* Product mix.
* Channel choice.
* Platform commission.
* Add-on structure.
* Photo quality.
* Perceived value.

Reducing price should not be the first solution.

---

## 28. Final Pricing Decision

Gift Story uses a variable pricing model.

The price of each box is based on:

* Actual cost.
* Packaging.
* Channel fees.
* Payment fees.
* Target profit.
* Brand perception.
* Category value.

The minimum target profit is:

**50% above total cost**

299 SAR remains a possible price tier, but not a fixed price for all boxes.

The official website should not show detailed product prices by default.
Prices should live inside active sales channels such as Salla, Zid, HungerStation, ToYou, The Chefz, or through quoted special requests.
