# Gift Story — Official Website

## 1. Purpose

This folder contains the planning, copy, wireframes, and references for the official Gift Story website.

The official website is the brand home.

It should present Gift Story as a premium gifting brand and guide customers to the active commerce platform.

The website should not be treated as a normal product store or a simple one-page checkout.

---

## 2. Website Role

The official website is responsible for:

* Introducing Gift Story.
* Explaining the brand story.
* Showing the gifting concept.
* Presenting the packaging experience.
* Introducing the active category.
* Showing Coming Soon categories.
* Displaying previous or sample stories.
* Hosting the Special Gift Story Request page.
* Showing policies.
* Providing official contact channels.
* Sending customers to the active commerce platform.

The website builds trust and desire.

The commerce platform completes the sale.

---

## 3. Commerce Platform Role

Salla, Zid, or any approved sales platform should handle:

* Active products.
* Prices.
* Inventory.
* Checkout.
* Payment.
* Orders.
* Invoices.
* Delivery settings.
* Customer notifications.

The official website should not duplicate the sales platform.

---

## 7. Pricing Rule

The official website should not show detailed active product prices by default.

Prices should appear inside the active sales channel only.

Reason:

Each sales channel may have different pricing due to:

* Payment fees.
* Platform fees.
* Delivery app commissions.
* Operational cost.
* Channel positioning.
* Special request differences.

The website should focus on the value and story, not price comparison.

---

## 8. Special Request Rule

Special requests should be handled through a dedicated page:

**طلب قصة خاصة**
**Special Gift Story Request**

The page should include:

* Explanation.
* Form.
* Preparation time note.
* Availability note.
* Contact consent.

Customer-facing note:

```text
الطلبات الخاصة تحتاج وقتًا إضافيًا للتجهيز، وتعتمد على توفر القطع المناسبة وإمكانية تنفيذ الفكرة.
```

## 11. Packaging Information

The website should clearly show that packaging is part of the Gift Story value.

Approved packaging structure:

* Branded outer gift bag.
* Branded inner box.
* Tissue paper.
* Sticker.
* Gift card.
* Protective nylon or shipping carton when needed.

Approved sizes:

```text
Inner box: 40 × 30 × 10 cm
Outer gift bag: 45 × 33 × 15 cm
```

Protective nylon or shipping carton is logistics protection only.
It is not part of the premium unboxing identity.

## 12. Print and Color Note

The website should include this note where relevant:

```text
قد تختلف بعض التفاصيل اللونية بين العروض الرقمية والتغليف المطبوع، حيث تعتمد Gift Story في التغليف الفعلي على طباعة فاخرة بلون ذهبي موحد حسب مواصفات الإنتاج.
```

This protects the brand from confusion between digital visuals and physical packaging.

## 13. Visual Direction

The website should feel:

* Premium.
* Soft.
* Warm.
* Calm.
* Spacious.
* Elegant.
* Story-driven.
* Gift-focused.

Approved visual direction:

* Off-white.
* Creamy beige.
* Nude.
* Muted / satin gold.
* Soft digital pink only when needed.
* Castle.
* Stars.
* Moon.
* Elegant packaging.

Avoid:

* Dark navy as the main identity.
* Loud colors.
* Heavy discount banners.
* Crowded grids.
* Too many icons.
* Random product photos.
* Cheap commercial visuals.
* Overly childish elements.

---

## 14. Image Rules

Use:

* Real packaging photos when available.
* Clean product photos.
* Soft mockups.
* Flat lay images.
* Detail shots.
* Category visuals.
* Previous story visuals.

Do not use:

* Raw supplier photos.
* Price tags.
* Discount labels.
* Store labels.
* Ross.
* MSRP.
* Dollar signs.
* Messy backgrounds.
* Low-quality screenshots.
* Personal information.

## 16. Website Copy Rules

Website copy should be:

* Arabic-first.
* Clear.
* Elegant.
* Warm.
* Practical.
* Short enough for mobile.
* Premium without exaggeration.

Avoid:

* Long poetic paragraphs.
* Heavy English.
* Repeated slogans.
* Discount language.
* Pushy sales language.
* Too many emojis.
* Confusing operational details.
* Overpromising availability or delivery speed.

---

## 17. Homepage Priority

The homepage should answer quickly:

1. What is Gift Story?
2. What makes it different?
3. What is the current story?
4. What does the packaging feel like?
5. How can the customer shop?
6. How can the customer request a special story?

If the homepage does not answer these questions clearly, it needs revision.

---

## 18. Mobile Priority

The website should be designed mobile-first.

Mobile priorities:

* Fast loading.
* Clear hero message.
* Clear CTA.
* Easy navigation.
* Short copy.
* Large readable text.
* Clean images.
* Simple forms.
* No clutter.

Most customers will likely discover Gift Story from Instagram, TikTok, or mobile links.

---

## 19. Technical Direction

The website can start as:

* Static website.
* Lightweight custom website.
* Cloudflare Pages deployment.
* GitHub repository.

The website should be:

* Easy to edit.
* Easy to deploy.
* Fast.
* SEO-friendly.
* Mobile-friendly.
* Connected to the commerce platform.

Avoid heavy unnecessary systems before launch.

The official website should make Gift Story feel like a premium brand.

It should not try to do everything.

The website is the story.

The commerce platform is the checkout.
